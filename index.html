1. Funding Events

Why it matters: New capital usually means new headcount, new tools, and new revenue pressure. Fintech and Regtech companies that have recently secured funding are actively looking to scale operations and improve efficiency.
Where to find it: Crunchbase, LinkedIn News, press releases, Pitchbook, CB Insights
Pro move: Prioritize companies raising Seed ‚Üí Series B (most GTM expansion). Companies that received funding in the last 6 months are 3x more likely to be evaluating new solutions.

2. Hiring for RevOps, Compliance, or Sales Roles

Why it matters: Teams hiring ops = scaling outbound, which means CRM and lead process chaos. Compliance hiring signals regulatory pressure and need for better systems.
Where to find it: LinkedIn Jobs, company career pages, job boards like Indeed and ZipRecruiter
Pro move: Look specifically for "Revenue Operations," "Sales Operations," "AML Compliance," or "Regulatory Compliance" roles. Companies hiring these positions are often experiencing growing pains in their current systems.

3. Tech Stack Changes

Why it matters: New tools = new workflows = new pain points. Companies implementing new systems are in active buying mode and looking to optimize their tech ecosystem.
Where to find it: BuiltWith, Wappalyzer, job descriptions, LinkedIn posts from employees
Pro move: Flag companies adding Hubspot, Salesforce, Pipedrive (CRMs), Stripe, Plaid (payment processing), or WooCommerce, Shopify, Big Commerce (e-commerce platforms). These integrations often trigger a cascade of other technology needs.

4. Regulatory Filings or Certifications

Why it matters: MSB licenses, SOC2, and compliance certifications drive urgent operational changes. Regulatory requirements create immediate needs for better systems and processes.
Where to find it: State registries, NMLS Consumer Access, Crunchbase alerts, company press releases
Pro move: RegTech buyers almost always move faster post-licensing. Companies that have recently obtained new licenses or certifications have an immediate need to ensure compliance, creating a perfect buying window.

5. Geographic Expansion

Why it matters: New markets mean new regulatory requirements and scaling challenges. Companies expanding to new regions need to adapt their systems quickly.
Where to find it: Press releases, LinkedIn announcements, job postings for new locations
Pro move: Look for companies opening offices in highly regulated markets (NY, CA, UK, Singapore). These expansions typically require significant operational adjustments and new compliance tools.

6. Leadership Changes

Why it matters: New executives often drive new initiatives and technology evaluations. They typically have a 90-day window to make an impact and are open to new solutions.
Where to find it: LinkedIn, press releases, company news pages
Pro move: Focus on new CTOs, CROs, COOs, and Heads of Growth. These roles are most likely to champion new systems and processes within the first 3-6 months of their tenure.

7. Product Launch or Feature Expansion

Why it matters: New offerings create new operational complexities and data management challenges. Companies scaling their product suite need better systems to manage growth.
Where to find it: Product Hunt, company blogs, press releases, social media announcements
Pro move: Companies that have launched new products in the last quarter are often struggling with their existing systems' ability to handle the increased complexity.

How To Use This System

Implementation Guide

1 Add these signals as fields in your CRM

Create custom fields in Hubspot, Salesforce, or Pipedrive to track each signal. Set up automation to flag when multiple signals are detected.

2 Score leads with stacked signals

1-2 signals = Medium priority
3+ signals = High priority
Certain combinations (e.g., recent funding + hiring RevOps) = Immediate outreach

3 Build outbound that matches the motion

Customize your outreach based on the specific signals detected. For example:

  • Funding event ‚Üí Focus on scaling efficiently
  • Compliance hiring ‚Üí Address regulatory challenges
  • Tech stack changes ‚Üí Discuss integration benefits
4 Route to the right rep

Ensure leads with the highest signal scores are routed to your best-performing reps who can capitalize on these time-sensitive opportunities.

5 Measure and refine

Track which signals or combinations of signals lead to the highest conversion rates and continuously refine your scoring model.
